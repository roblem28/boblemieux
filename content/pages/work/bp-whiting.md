I served as Project Controls Manager at Fluor Enterprises on BP's Whiting Refinery Modernization in Whiting, Indiana, responsible for scheduling and earned value across the program. The project involved major process-unit upgrades at one of the largest refineries in the Midwest — including a new 102,000 BPD coker, hydroprocessing-related improvements, and state-of-the-art environmental controls for water treatment and air emissions. Publicly reported project value was approximately $3.8B–$4.0B.

**Role — Project Controls Manager, Scheduling and Earned Value, Fluor Enterprises.** I owned scheduling and EVM across the EPC scope: integrated master schedule development and baseline management, multi-discipline schedule integration across engineering, procurement, and construction, earned value measurement and performance reporting, and change control. On a program of this complexity, earned value is the mechanism that connects physical progress to cost exposure — and gives the owner the quantitative basis to make decisions about risk, forecast, and corrective action.

The modernization was designed to increase the refinery's ability to process heavy crude and extend its long-term capability. The new coker — the last major milestone — came on stream in mid-November of its commissioning year, with BP subsequently reporting an expected incremental $1 billion of operating cash flow per year from the reconfigured facility. The project was also the largest private sector investment in Indiana history.
