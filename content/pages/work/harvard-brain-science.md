I served on the large Harvard Northwest Science Building project, a major multidisciplinary research facility in Cambridge designed to support advanced laboratory, classroom, and office environments. The building houses neuroscience and related scientific programs, including the Center for Brain Science, within a larger research complex built for flexible, high-performance academic science.

**Role — Owner's Representative, Project Controls.** Schedule tracking and alignment across multiple stakeholders, cost control and forecasting, coordination between design teams, construction teams, and end users, and support for technical systems integration within active lab environments.

The project's complexity came from high-performance MEP systems, specialized research equipment integration, ongoing program changes driven by research needs, and multiple stakeholders with competing priorities — all within a requirement for long-term flexibility in lab configurations.
